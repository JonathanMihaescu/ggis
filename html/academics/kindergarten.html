Curriculum

Our kindergarten program provides children with meaningful opportunities to explore, discover and develop their God-given gifts and interests. Children sing, move, play daily as part of their learning experience.

During the two years of kindergarten children are slowly introduced to the world of letters and sounds. They begin to recognize them and match the letter to its sound. Through play and repetition on a daily basis, our kids are able to read 4 to 5 letter words and even read short sentences or stories at the end of kindergarten. Numeracy - recognizing numbers up to 20, count and even simple notions of addition and subtraction are taught during these years. Practicing daily Handwriting by tracing letters, words and numbers will help improve their fine motor abilities and visual-motor performance.

The Abeka Reading Program is an important part of our daily schedule where children learn to read short sentences and little stories. Besides this the teacher reads stories out loud for the children to inspire the love for reading. Discussions follow up the story time for reading comprehension. Children have multiple occasions to practice English when they play, socialize, negotiate, share, cooperate etc. Show and tell is a fun class when they present in front of the class their favorite toy, game, book, food, experience, pet etc.

Bible stories presented at their level of understanding complete our curriculum. In addition to these children develop a wide range of skills during art, music and sport classes.
