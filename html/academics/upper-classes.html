Secondary

The Secondary consists of middle school and high school classes that aim to prepare students for college and university. These classes are designed to address the holistic needs of the students during this important time of their life. With a heavy emphasis on critical thinking and application of what they’ve learned, students are encouraged to grew into leaders and doers in their class and community.

The middle school grades mark a “transition to independence,” for the students. As they change classrooms each period, students are expected to be organized, disciplined, and prepared for each class they attend. The core classes during these years are: Math, English, History, and Science, but the students also have elective classes that consist of: Music, Physical Education, Shop, IT, Art, Personal Finance, and a “Masterclass,” that allows them to perform a “deep dive” into subjects that interest them. All of these classes are taught with the goal to provide the students with a mastery learning level of the subjects. These years are crucial to the physical, spiritual, and academic development of the student as they grow into young adults.

During high school, the students continue with the core classes of: Math, English, History, and Science as well as Worldview Bible class every year that they attend our school. They have opportunities through their elective classes, such as: Music class and Band, Physical Education and Health, IT Classes and Programming, Foreign Languages, Shop and Construction classes, as well as SAT and college prep classes, MLA/APA writing classes, Vocabulary, and Leadership and Entrepreneurship classes. The high school students have the opportunity to gain practical experience through our Volunteer Program and Internship opportunities that arise through the Entrepreneurship and Masterclass. There are field trips and class trips planned during the year that allow the students to experience learning outside the classroom, outside the city, and even outside the country.
